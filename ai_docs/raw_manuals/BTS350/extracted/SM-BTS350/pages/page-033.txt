Service manual
5. - Maintenance
5.1. - General care
In order to ensure optimal operation of this instrument it is necessary to follow some minimal maintenance rules.
• 
Never use detergents or abrasive products for cleaning the outside of the instrument. Use only a cloth dampened in 
water and neutral soap.
• 
If a reagent or any corrosive product is spilt on the apparatus, clean it immediately with a damp cloth and water.
• 
The cuvette holder tray is equipped with a watertight joint in order to prevent penetration of liquid into the inner part 
of the instrument. If liquid is spilt onto the tray, wipe it with a damp cloth and water. 
• 
If a cuvette breaks in the cuvette holder or liquid pours into it for any reason, the holder has a drainage hole connected 
to the outside. However, distilled water must be passed through it, and then the inside dried.
5.2. - Cleaning of the optical components
The following indications should be taken into account for the cleaning of the optical components:
• 
Optical components to be considered: lenses, filters and photodiodes.
• 
Recommended material: 
• 
WYPALL LITE paper (56 gr/m2) SCOTT.
• 
50% alcohol + ether solution.
• 
Cotton buds.
• 
Blower.
• 
To ensure correct handling of the optical components, please take the following precautions:
• 
The area for manipulating the instrument should be clean and tidy.
• 
As the components are fragile, they should be treated carefully; a fall could result in breakage.
• 
Avoid touching the active parts with your fingers.
• 
Lenses, filters and photodiode should be held by their sides, LEDs and photodiodes by the connecting terminals.
• 
To clean the components, first remove the dust using the blower; thus scratches caused by small particles on the 
surface can be avoided, when rubbing with paper.
• 
In the case of persistent or greasy dirt, clean with a paper soaked with the alcohol/ether solution and then with 
a dry paper. Sometimes, for instance when cleaning the filters or the photodiode window, cotton buds may be 
helpful together with the paper in the most delicate parts.
• 
After any cleaning it is convenient to repeat blowing with the blower thus eliminating any residual paper or 
cotton nap.
• 
When mounting or dismounting any component take into account the corresponding tools and procedures, as 
they are designed to avoid manipulation problems.
5.3. - Cleaning the filters
• 
Dismantle the top housing of the semi-automatic analyzer.
• 
Dismantle the optical bench of the electronic board.
• 
Remove the optical bench filters at the lower part of the bench.
• 
Clean them as indicated in section 5.2.
• 
Reassemble the filters in their holders and the latter on the optical bench.
