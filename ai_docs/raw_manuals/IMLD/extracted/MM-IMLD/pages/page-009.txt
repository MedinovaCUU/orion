4. Cleaning
Necessary material:
- A mixture of 70% alcohol and 30% ether.
- Lens cleaning tissue.
- Rubber bulb for blowing air.
- Fine, soft brush.
- Cotton tipped.
Procedure
a. Cleaning the body of the microscope.
- Use a cloth moistened with a solution of water and neu-
tral soap. Never use detergents or abrasives.
b. Cleaning of the microscope's external components.
- General rules
 Once the external optical components have been remo-
ved (lenses, condenser, objectives), proceed as follows:
- Use the fine, soft brush to eliminate dust and other
particles. Use the rubber bulb to assure that all dust is
removed. To eliminate adhered dust, use the lens cleaning
paper by wiping in a circular motion. If there are any
fingerprints or remains of grease, use a cotton swab
moistened with the alcohol/ether solution. Caution: Do not
use the alcohol/ether solution to clean the daylight filter.
Instead, use distilled water and dry with a soft cloth.
c. Cleaning the internal lenses.
 - If dirt is detected on the internal lenses, they must be
taken off and be cleaned with the fine brush and compressed
air (or using the rubber bulb). It is not advisable to remove
the optical elements, given that they would have to be
adjusted again.
